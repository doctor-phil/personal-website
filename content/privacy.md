_Last updated: June 25, 2026_

This website ([psolimine.net](https://www.psolimine.net/)) is the personal
academic site of Philip Solimine. This page explains what data the site
collects and why.

## What is collected

**Server access logs.** Like virtually every website, this site records
standard request metadata for each visit. This is handled at the hosting/edge
layer and includes:

- your IP address,
- your browser's user-agent string,
- the page or file you requested and the referring URL (if any),
- your browser's language preference,
- an approximate location (country/region/city) derived from your IP,
- the date and time of the request.

This information is sent automatically by your browser with every HTTP
request. Collecting it does not place any cookie or script on your device.

**Analytics.** This site uses **Google Analytics 4** to understand aggregate
readership (which pages are read, roughly where visitors come from). Google
Analytics sets cookies and processes usage data on Google's infrastructure.
You can opt out using Google's
[browser add-on](https://tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout) or by blocking
analytics cookies.

## Why it is collected

- **Operating and securing the site** — detecting and mitigating abusive or
  automated traffic (bots, scrapers, scanners) and diagnosing problems.
- **Understanding readership** — basic, aggregate insight into which research
  and teaching materials are useful.

Under the EU/UK GDPR, the lawful basis for access logging and basic analytics
is **legitimate interest** in running and protecting the site.

## Who processes it

- **Netlify** hosts the site and processes requests (incl. access logs).
- **Cloudflare** may process requests for performance and bot/abuse mitigation.
- **Google** processes analytics data.

Each operates under its own privacy policy.

## Retention

Access logs are retained only as long as necessary for security and
operational purposes. Analytics data is retained per the configured Google
Analytics retention settings.

## Your choices and rights

You can block cookies and analytics in your browser, and (for EU/UK/California
residents, among others) you may have rights to access or request deletion of
personal data associated with you. To make a request, contact me via the links
on the [About page](/about/#contact).
